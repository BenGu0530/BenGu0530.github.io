I'm a first-year M.S. student in Robotics at Carnegie Mellon, advised by Aaron Johnson in the Robomechanics Lab. I finished my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at CMU in May 2025.

My research sits at the intersection of legged locomotion, reinforcement learning, and optimal control. I build minimally-actuated bipedal robots — designing the mechanism, the controller, and the learning pipeline end-to-end — and I care a lot about the sim-to-real loop that makes those policies actually walk on hardware.

Right now I'm working on a penguin-inspired biped for low-friction slopes, and continuing work on Mugatu, the first steerable single-motor biped. I'm targeting ICRA 2027 as lead author.
